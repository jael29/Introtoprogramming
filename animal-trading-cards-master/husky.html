Siberian Husky

Huskies have a thick double coat that keeps them well insulated. Their undercoat is short and warm, while the overcoat is long and water-resistant. Their almond-shaped eyes allow them to squint to keep out snow. Huskies will wrap their tails around their faces while they sleep; their breath warms the tail and keeps the nose and face protected from the cold.

  • Their Nature: Huskies are born to run.
  • Success Debut: Their skills impressed Alaskans.
  • How they stay warm: A lot of features help keep them warm
  • Saving Lives: A group saved a small town in Alaska

The Siberian Husky is a medium-sized working dog, quick and light on his feet and free and graceful in action. His moderately compact and well furred body, erect ears and brush tail suggest his Northern heritage. His characteristic gait is smooth and seemingly effortless.
